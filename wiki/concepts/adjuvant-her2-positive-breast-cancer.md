# Adjuvant therapy for HER2-positive early breast cancer

## Explanation

Adjuvant therapy decisions in [[her2-positive-breast-cancer]] depend primarily on **pathologic response to neoadjuvant therapy**:

- **pCR (pathologic complete response):** complete one year of HER2 blockade. Trastuzumab alone if node-negative at presentation; add pertuzumab if node-positive.
- **Residual invasive disease (non-pCR):** switch to a HER2-directed ADC. This is the higher-risk pathway and the one Q3 lives in.

For HR+/HER2+ disease, endocrine therapy runs in parallel with HER2-directed adjuvant therapy.

## Key points

- **Current standard for residual disease: [[trastuzumab-emtansine]] × 14 cycles** per [[katherine-trial]] (50% IDFS reduction vs trastuzumab; 8.4-year follow-up confirmed sustained IDFS and OS benefit) [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Emerging standard: [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]]** per [[destiny-breast05]] — 53% IDFS reduction vs T-DM1, brain-met risk reduction, immature OS trend favorable. **PDUFA July 7, 2026** for adjuvant indication [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **The trade-off in moving from T-DM1 to T-DXd: ILD/pneumonitis** (9.6% vs 1.6%, including some Grade 5 events). Patients with baseline pulmonary disease, prior chest RT, or limited steroid tolerance may still favor T-DM1 even after T-DXd approval [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **HR status does not change the HER2-directed agent choice** in residual disease — benefit of T-DXd over T-DM1 was consistent across HR+ and HR− subsets in [[destiny-breast05]] [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **No established role for [[tucatinib]]** in the adjuvant residual-disease setting. [[her2climb-05]] is metastatic 1L maintenance, not adjuvant — extending those data to adjuvant use is investigational extrapolation [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **ctDNA MRD-positivity does not yet alter standard adjuvant choice** per current guidelines — see [[mrd-guided-therapy-escalation]] for the rationale and the open clinical question.

## Decision skeleton (high-risk-within-high-risk: residual disease + MRD+)

1. **Is T-DXd accessible (post-PDUFA, on-label, payer-approved)?** If yes, T-DXd is the preferred HER2-directed adjuvant agent based on [[destiny-breast05]] superiority — unless ILD risk dominates.
2. **Is the ILD risk profile acceptable?** Baseline PFTs, prior chest RT history, autoimmune/pulmonary comorbidities. If high ILD risk, T-DM1 remains a reasonable choice.
3. **MRD-guided escalation is investigational.** The Q3 case (residual + MRD+) is the population where escalation pressure is greatest, but no Phase 3 data support a specific escalation strategy. See [[mrd-guided-therapy-escalation]].
4. **Endocrine therapy** runs in parallel for HR+/HER2+ disease.
