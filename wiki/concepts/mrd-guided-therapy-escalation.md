# MRD-guided adjuvant therapy escalation

## Explanation

Using a sensitive biomarker — typically [[circulating-tumor-dna]] — to detect molecular minimal residual disease after definitive local therapy, then **escalating adjuvant systemic therapy** in MRD+ patients in hopes of preventing clinical recurrence. The clinical premise is sound: ctDNA detection is robustly prognostic. The clinical execution remains **investigational** because no Phase 3 trial has yet shown that intervening on MRD-positivity *changes outcomes* vs standard adjuvant therapy alone.

## Key points

- **Prognostic vs predictive distinction.** ctDNA detection is **prognostic** (it identifies higher recurrence risk) but not yet **predictive** (we lack evidence that any particular escalation strategy in MRD+ patients improves outcomes) [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Current guideline status (NCCN, ASCO, ESMO, early 2026):** **do not recommend** routine ctDNA MRD testing to guide adjuvant escalation in HR+/HER2+ disease [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **The [[zest-trial]] cautionary tale.** The major Phase 3 designed to test MRD-guided escalation closed early due to randomization difficulties. Lesson: **adequate event rates require enrichment for higher baseline risk**, not just MRD-positivity in an unselected population.
- **Where the equipoise lives now: high-risk within high-risk** — patients who already have residual disease post-neoadjuvant *and* are MRD+. This is the [[residual-disease-vs-mrd-positivity]] nested-stratum problem; it's exactly the Q3 scenario.
- **Active prospective trials:** NCT07136493 (early-stage breast cancer including HER2+) is enrolling to associate ctDNA detectability with recurrence-free survival — **observational/correlative**, not yet a randomized escalation trial [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]]. **MRD clearance** is being explored as a surrogate endpoint for ctDNA-guided adjuvant therapy.
- **Off-label escalation strategies in current practice** (no data, judgment-based): switching [[trastuzumab-emtansine]] → [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]] off-label pre-PDUFA; adding [[tucatinib]] off-label by extrapolating from metastatic data. **Both are investigational and should be presented as such to the patient.**

## What the wiki cannot tell you

Whether to escalate in an MRD+ patient who is already on guideline-concordant adjuvant therapy. The data don't say. The decision is the user's clinical judgment plus patient values — which makes it an **avatar entry**, not a wiki claim. See `wiki/avatar/{user}/decisions.md`.
