# Residual disease vs MRD-positivity

## Explanation

These two terms are often used interchangeably in casual oncology discussion, but they refer to **distinct, nested risk strata** measured by different modalities at different time points. Conflating them produces incorrect clinical reasoning — most acutely in HER2+ breast cancer post-neoadjuvant TCHP, where both can apply to the same patient simultaneously.

## Definitions

| Term | Modality | Timing | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Residual disease (non-pCR)** | Histopathology of resection specimen | At surgery, after neoadjuvant therapy | Anatomically detectable invasive tumor remaining in breast or nodes despite neoadjuvant systemic therapy |
| **MRD-positivity** | [[circulating-tumor-dna]] in plasma | Post-surgery, pre/post adjuvant therapy | Molecular evidence of tumor DNA in circulation in the absence of overt anatomic disease |

## Key points

- **Residual disease defined [[katherine-trial]] eligibility.** It identifies patients who failed to achieve pCR — a known higher-risk group for whom adjuvant T-DM1 (and now T-DXd) demonstrably improves outcomes [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **MRD-positivity is an additional, finer-grained risk biomarker.** It can stratify patients with residual disease *further* — those with residual disease *and* detectable ctDNA are at higher recurrence risk than those with residual disease alone.
- **The strata are nested, not overlapping:**
  - Residual disease, MRD− → standard risk for the residual-disease group (still high)
  - Residual disease, MRD+ → highest-risk subset (the Q3 scenario)
  - pCR, MRD+ → clinically interesting but rare; intermediate management uncertainty
  - pCR, MRD− → lowest residual risk
- **The Gemini search summary [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]] partially conflated these terms** — treating them as overlapping rather than nested. The wiki disambiguates them here so future queries inherit the correct framing.
- **Clinical implication:** the evidence base for *what to do* in residual disease (KATHERINE, DESTINY-Breast05) is robust. The evidence base for *what to do additionally* in MRD+ residual disease is **absent** — see [[mrd-guided-therapy-escalation]]. This is a real gap, not a wiki oversight.
