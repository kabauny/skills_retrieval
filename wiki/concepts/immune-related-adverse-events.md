# Immune-related adverse events (irAEs)

## Explanation

Off-target inflammatory toxicities caused by checkpoint-inhibitor-mediated activation of T cells against host tissues. Mechanism: PD-1/PD-L1 (or CTLA-4) blockade lifts the brake on T cells, restoring anti-tumor activity but also enabling autoimmune-like attack on healthy organs. Distinct from chemotherapy toxicity in mechanism, time course, and management — **immunosuppression (corticosteroids ± biologic agents) is the treatment**, not dose reduction or supportive care alone.

## Key points

- **Common organs affected:**
  - **GI (colitis, hepatitis)** — colitis is the prototype Grade ≥3 irAE; ~32% of severe irAEs in [[keynote-522]] [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **Endocrine** (thyroiditis, hypophysitis, adrenalitis, type 1 diabetes) — often *irreversible*, managed with hormone replacement; ICI may be continued in well-controlled cases
  - **Pulmonary** (pneumonitis) — ~23% recurrence on re-challenge specifically [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **Skin** (rash, vitiligo, severe variants like SJS/TEN)
  - **Hepatic, renal, cardiac, neurologic** — less common but consequential
- **Time course:** can occur during therapy or months after discontinuation. Sequential, multi-organ irAEs reported including post-cessation [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Severe (Grade ≥3) management protocol:**
  1. **Hold** the checkpoint inhibitor
  2. **High-dose corticosteroids** — prednisone 1–2 mg/kg/day or IV methylprednisolone [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  3. **No improvement in 2–3 days → escalate to biologic** (e.g., [[infliximab]] for steroid-refractory colitis)
  4. **Multidisciplinary specialty consultation** (GI, endocrine, pulm, etc.)
  5. **Slow steroid taper** over 4–6 weeks
- **Frequency in [[keynote-522]]:** Grade ≥3 irAEs in 13.0% (pembro arm) vs 1.5% (placebo); real-world 13.6% [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]. Adjuvant-phase irAE rate 18.1% in real-world data — meaning continuing pembrolizumab after the neoadjuvant phase carries non-trivial ongoing risk [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Fatal events:** rare but reported in [[keynote-522]] — adrenal crisis, autoimmune encephalitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, sepsis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Re-challenge:** see [[pembrolizumab-rechallenge-after-severe-irae]] for the structured calculus.

## Distinction from chemotherapy toxicity

| Aspect | Chemotherapy toxicity | irAE |
|---|---|---|
| Mechanism | Direct cytotoxicity to dividing cells | Off-target T-cell activation |
| Onset | Typically predictable per cycle | Can be late, even post-cessation |
| Treatment | Dose reduction, growth factors, supportive care | **Immunosuppression** (steroids, biologics) |
| Resolution | Usually reversible with discontinuation | Often persistent; some endocrinopathies are permanent |
| Implications for re-treatment | Often reduce dose and continue | More complex — see [[pembrolizumab-rechallenge-after-severe-irae]] |
