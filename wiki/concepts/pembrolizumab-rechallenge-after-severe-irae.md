# Pembrolizumab re-challenge after severe irAE

## Explanation

The clinical question of whether to **re-challenge** a patient with [[pembrolizumab]] after a severe (Grade ≥3) [[immune-related-adverse-events|immune-related adverse event]] led to ICI suspension. Distinct from the de-escalation question in untreated patients — re-challenge is a **reactive** decision in a patient who has already declared themselves susceptible to severe immune toxicity. This concept page captures the framework; the **adjuvant-pembrolizumab-after-pCR specific application** for the Q5 case lives in [[adjuvant-pembrolizumab-after-pcr-tnbc]].

## Key points

- **Real-world re-challenge data (September 2025) in TNBC patients on the [[keynote-522]] regimen:**
  - **53% successfully re-challenged** at all [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **16% required permanent discontinuation** — the irAE was severe enough that re-challenge wasn't attempted [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **Re-challenge candidates were typically:** younger, good performance status, **lower-grade (Grade 1–2) initial toxicity** [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]] — note this excludes the Q5 patient profile
- **Recurrence risk on re-challenge:**
  - **28.8% irAE recurrence rate** across irAE types and tumor types [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **~23% specifically for pneumonitis or colitis** — directly relevant to Q5 (the patient had Grade 3 colitis) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - Recurrent irAEs are often more severe than the initial event
- **Sequential, multi-organ irAEs** can occur — the first irAE doesn't predict which organ a second will hit. Vigilance must extend across systems [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Endocrinopathy exception:** hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, and other endocrinopathies are often **irreversible but stable** with hormone replacement; ICI re-challenge is more readily considered for endocrine-only irAEs than for colitis or pneumonitis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].

## The structured calculus

Re-challenge weighs three quantities:

1. **Marginal benefit of resuming therapy** in this specific patient and clinical context (e.g., for adjuvant pembrolizumab after pCR in TNBC, the marginal OS benefit is ~0.7% — see [[adjuvant-pembrolizumab-after-pcr-tnbc]])
2. **Recurrence risk** of the prior irAE (~23% for colitis/pneumonitis specifically)
3. **Severity of recurrence** — often equal to or greater than initial event

If marginal benefit is small (e.g., adjuvant after pCR) and recurrence risk is meaningful, re-challenge has limited rationale. If marginal benefit is large (e.g., metastatic disease responding to IO with no alternative) and prior irAE was lower-grade, re-challenge is more defensible.

## Q5 patient profile

- Initial irAE: **Grade 3 colitis** (steroid + infliximab requirement) — this is the high-severity end of the re-challenge population
- Marginal benefit of completing adjuvant pembrolizumab: **small in pCR patients** (~0.7% absolute 5-yr OS gap) — see [[adjuvant-pembrolizumab-after-pcr-tnbc]]
- Recurrence risk: ~23% specifically for colitis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
- **Overall calculus tilts toward omission** for this specific patient profile — but no Phase 3 evidence directly answers the question. **Avatar territory.**

## What the wiki cannot tell you

Whether to re-challenge a specific Q5-style patient. The data describe population-level frequencies; the decision is shaped by patient values (intolerance for any recurrence vs. desire for maximal curative-intent therapy), comorbidities (steroid tolerance, baseline GI status), and the patient's stated preferences after informed discussion. **Capture in `wiki/avatar/{user}/decisions.md`.**
