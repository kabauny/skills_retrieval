# Source summary: TNBC pCR after KEYNOTE-522 with severe irAE — adjuvant pembrolizumab continuation vs omission (2025–2026)

## Summary

A Gemini grounded search synthesizing 29 sources on the KEYNOTE-522 regimen in [[triple-negative-breast-cancer]] (TNBC), with focus on the Q5 question: a patient who achieves pCR but develops Grade 3+ immune-mediated colitis requiring steroids and infliximab during the neoadjuvant phase — re-challenge with adjuvant pembrolizumab to maximize curative intent, or omit knowing pCR already portends excellent prognosis? The search supports a real-world reading of the trade-off but does **not** definitively answer the question — the trial wasn't designed to isolate adjuvant benefit after pCR.

## Key findings

- **KEYNOTE-522 design:** neoadjuvant pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (carboplatin + paclitaxel → AC) × 8 cycles, then surgery, then adjuvant pembrolizumab × 9 cycles (~1 year), regardless of pCR status. Population: high-risk early TNBC (Stage II–III, T1cN1–2, T2–4N0) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Efficacy (5-year follow-up, ESMO 2024):**
  - **pCR rate:** 64.8% (pembro arm) vs 51.2% (placebo) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **5-year OS overall:** 86.6% vs 81.7%, **HR 0.66 (95% CI 0.50–0.87, P=0.0015)** [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **5-year OS in pCR subgroup:** 95.1% (pembro) vs 94.4% (placebo) — **small absolute benefit (~0.7%)**, suggesting most of the OS gain is in the non-pCR subset [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **"Road to pCR may matter":** the regimen's benefit may derive partly from the chemo-IO combination during neoadjuvant phase establishing immune memory — the adjuvant component's marginal contribution after pCR is uncertain [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
- **Toxicity:**
  - **Grade ≥3 irAEs:** 13.0% (pembro) vs 1.5% (placebo) in the trial; 13.6% in real-world data [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **GI predominance:** ~32% of Grade ≥3 irAEs were gastrointestinal (colitis the prototype) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **Adjuvant-phase irAE rate:** 18.1% in real-world data (April 2025) — relevant to the "should we continue?" calculus [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **Fatal events:** rare but reported — adrenal crisis, autoimmune encephalitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, sepsis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
- **Re-challenge after irAE (September 2025 real-world):**
  - **53% successfully re-challenged**; permanent discontinuation in 16% [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - Re-challenge candidates: typically younger, good performance status, **lower-grade (Grade 1–2) initial toxicity** [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - **28.8% irAE recurrence rate** on re-challenge (any irAE, across tumor types); ~23% recurrence specifically for pneumonitis/colitis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
- **Severe irAE management protocol** (Q5-relevant — patient already had Grade 3 colitis on infliximab):
  - Hold ICI; high-dose corticosteroids (prednisone 1–2 mg/kg/day or IV methylprednisolone)
  - **No improvement in 2–3 days → infliximab single dose**, taper steroids over 4–6 weeks
  - Multidisciplinary GI consultation [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
- **Biomarkers (exploratory, not actionable for de-escalation):** TMB and T-cell inflamed 18-gene expression profile (TcellinfGEP) prognostic for pCR/EFS, but pembrolizumab benefit was observed regardless of these markers in the overall population. **No biomarker-guided de-escalation in untreated patients is current standard** [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Active de-escalation trials:**
  - **SCARLET** — anthracycline-sparing
  - **OptimICE-RD, TROPION-Breast03** — Trop-2 ADCs adjuvantly for residual disease
  - **NCT07327021** (started Jan 2026) — MRI + ctDNA-MRD-guided de-escalation
- **NCCN/ASCO/ESMO guidance:** full KEYNOTE-522 regimen including 1 year adjuvant pembrolizumab is recommended for all eligible high-risk TNBC, **including pCR patients** — the trial population was not pre-stratified by pCR for treatment decisions, and de-escalation in pCR patients is **not yet endorsed** outside trials [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].

## The Q5 disambiguation

The search distinguishes two related but distinct decisions, and the wiki should preserve the distinction:

1. **Reactive de-escalation after irAE** (the Q5 case): patient *already had* Grade 3 colitis. The decision is whether the recurrence risk on re-challenge (23–29% irAE recurrence) is acceptable given the small marginal OS benefit from adjuvant pembrolizumab in pCR patients (~0.7% absolute).
2. **Proactive biomarker-guided de-escalation** in untreated patients: identifying who can safely *start* with less. Not yet established; NCT07327021, SCARLET ongoing.

The Q5 case lives in (1). Real-world re-challenge data is the most directly relevant evidence; the trial design itself does not isolate this decision.

## Limitations / caveats

- **Secondary source.** Gemini synthesis. Primary KEYNOTE-522 publications (NEJM 2020, ESMO 2024 OS update) should land in `raw/papers/`.
- **Real-world data heavily cited.** Re-challenge percentages and adjuvant-phase irAE rates are from observational analyses, not RCT data. Useful but evidence-level distinct.
- **The pCR-specific OS gap (~0.7% absolute) is from a subgroup analysis** — not the trial's primary endpoint. Treat as hypothesis-generating, not definitive.
- **Citation-insertion artifacts** in the Gemini text (sentence fragments split across citation tags) — same cosmetic issue seen in earlier searches; substance intact.
- **Some sources are sponsor (Merck, AstraZeneca for related agents) press releases** — flag when citing.
- **No specific NCCN/ASCO/ESMO guidance on omitting adjuvant pembrolizumab after severe irAE** — the guidance covers the regimen for all eligible patients but does not prescribe the irAE-driven discontinuation decision. That is, by design, judgment territory.
