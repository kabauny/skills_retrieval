# HR-positive HER2-negative breast cancer

## Overview

Most common breast cancer subtype (~70% of cases). Defined by hormone receptor positivity (ER and/or PR) and absence of HER2 overexpression/amplification. Driven primarily by estrogen signaling, which makes endocrine therapy the cornerstone of management. Distinct from [[her2-positive-breast-cancer]] in mechanism, prognosis, and treatment paradigm.

## Key facts

- **Adjuvant treatment paradigm depends on:**
  - **Anatomic stage** (T, N) — guides whether chemotherapy is even on the table
  - **Genomic risk** — most often [[oncotype-dx-recurrence-score]]
  - **Menopausal status** — pivotal because endocrine therapy options differ ([[ovarian-function-suppression]] is required in premenopausal women using [[aromatase-inhibitor]])
  - **Clinical features** — Grade, tumor size, age — modify decisions especially in the genomic intermediate-risk zone
- **Endocrine therapy backbone:**
  - **Postmenopausal:** AI alone for 5–10 years (or [[tamoxifen]] if AI intolerant)
  - **Premenopausal:** [[tamoxifen]] (with or without OFS) **or** [[aromatase-inhibitor]] **with [[ovarian-function-suppression]]**. AI requires OFS in premenopausal women.
  - **High-risk premenopausal:** OFS + AI per [[soft-text-trials|SOFT/TEXT]] outperforms OFS + tamoxifen [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Adjuvant chemotherapy decision in early disease:**
  - **Node-negative:** governed by [[tailorx-trial]] — RS guides need for chemotherapy. RS ≤10: skip. RS ≥26: include. RS 11–25 ("intermediate"): individualized; **premenopausal ≤50 with high-risk clinical features** derive small but persistent benefit.
  - **Node-positive (1–3 nodes):** governed by [[rxponder-trial]] — postmenopausal women derive no chemo benefit; **premenopausal women do** (40% iDFS-event RR reduction), with active debate over whether this is direct cytotoxicity or [[chemotherapy-induced-ovarian-suppression]].
- **Q4 patient subtype:** 39yo, ER+/PR+, HER2−, **node-negative**, RS 22, Grade 3, 2.5 cm. Premenopausal high-risk-clinical-features intermediate-RS — directly in [[tailorx-trial]] subgroup, philosophically informed by [[rxponder-trial]] debate.
- **HR+/HER2− vs HR+/HER2+:** different management entirely. HR+/HER2+ uses HER2-directed adjuvant therapy ([[trastuzumab-emtansine]] or [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]]) plus endocrine therapy in parallel — see [[her2-positive-breast-cancer]] and [[adjuvant-her2-positive-breast-cancer]].
- **Premenopausal disease characteristics:** often more advanced stage at diagnosis, higher tumor grades, larger tumor sizes, and more positive lymph nodes than postmenopausal disease — biological reason to treat the population specifically rather than extrapolating directly from postmenopausal trials [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
