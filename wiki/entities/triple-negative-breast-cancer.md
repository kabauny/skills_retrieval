# Triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC)

## Overview

Breast cancer subtype defined by absence of ER, PR, and HER2 expression. Comprises ~10–15% of breast cancers. Historically the most aggressive subtype with the fewest targeted treatment options — endocrine therapy and HER2-directed therapy are both inapplicable. Chemo-immunotherapy (the [[keynote-522]] regimen) is the current adjuvant standard for high-risk early disease.

## Key facts

- **Defining receptor profile:** ER−, PR−, HER2− — by IHC and/or in situ hybridization. Low-positive ER (1–10%) is sometimes managed similarly to TNBC depending on the primary tumor's overall biology.
- **Risk profile:** more aggressive than HR+/HER2− or HER2+ subtypes. Higher early recurrence rate (peak in years 1–3 post-diagnosis), higher CNS metastasis risk, more chemotherapy-sensitive but less endocrine- or HER2-targeted-therapy-amenable.
- **Standard early-stage treatment paradigm (high-risk Stage II–III, T1cN1–2 or T2–4N0):**
  - Neoadjuvant chemo-immunotherapy per [[keynote-522]] regimen ([[pembrolizumab]] + carboplatin + paclitaxel → AC × 8 cycles)
  - Surgery (lumpectomy or mastectomy with axillary management)
  - **Adjuvant pembrolizumab** × 9 cycles (~1 year), regardless of pCR — current guideline recommendation
  - For patients with **residual disease** post-neoadjuvant: capecitabine option (CREATE-X) or olaparib for germline BRCA1/2 mutations (OlympiA)
- **pCR rate after [[keynote-522]] regimen:** 64.8% (vs 51.2% with chemo alone) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]. pCR is a strong prognostic indicator — 5-year OS ~95% in pCR patients vs lower in non-pCR.
- **Q5 patient context:** Stage II–III TNBC who completed neoadjuvant KEYNOTE-522, achieved pCR, but developed Grade 3 immune-mediated colitis requiring steroids and infliximab during the neoadjuvant phase. Clinical equipoise on whether to complete the year of adjuvant pembrolizumab. See [[adjuvant-pembrolizumab-after-pcr-tnbc]].
- **Active de-escalation research:** SCARLET (anthracycline-sparing), OptimICE-RD and TROPION-Breast03 (Trop-2 ADCs adjuvantly for residual disease), NCT07327021 (MRI + ctDNA-guided de-escalation, started Jan 2026) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
