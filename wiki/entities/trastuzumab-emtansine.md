# Trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1)

## Overview

Antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) consisting of trastuzumab covalently linked to the cytotoxic agent emtansine (DM1, a maytansine derivative; tubulin inhibitor). FDA-approved for HER2-positive breast cancer in both metastatic (post-trastuzumab progression) and adjuvant (residual invasive disease after neoadjuvant therapy) settings.

## Key facts

- **Adjuvant indication: HER2+ residual invasive disease post-neoadjuvant chemotherapy** — established by [[katherine-trial]]: 50% reduction in IDFS event risk vs trastuzumab; long-term (8.4-year) follow-up confirmed sustained IDFS and improved OS [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Current guideline status:** NCCN Category 1 for HER2+ residual disease; supported by ASCO and ESMO [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Likely supersession by [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]]** in the adjuvant setting based on [[destiny-breast05]] — T-DM1 had 3-year IDFS 83.7% vs T-DXd 92.4% (HR 0.47 favoring T-DXd) [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Standard adjuvant dose:** 3.6 mg/kg IV q3w × 14 cycles per [[katherine-trial]] schedule [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Toxicity profile relative to T-DXd:** much lower interstitial lung disease (ILD) risk — 1.6% in [[destiny-breast05]] vs 9.6% with T-DXd [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]]. This is the principal trade-off favoring T-DM1 in patients with baseline pulmonary risk.
