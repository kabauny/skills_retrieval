# SOFT and TEXT trials

## Overview

Two parallel Phase 3 trials run by the International Breast Cancer Study Group that together established the role of [[ovarian-function-suppression]] (OFS) plus an [[aromatase-inhibitor]] in adjuvant therapy for premenopausal HR+ early breast cancer. **The pivotal evidence base for OFS+AI** in higher-risk premenopausal disease — directly anchors the "OFS+AI" arm of the Q4 decision.

## Key facts

- **SOFT (Suppression of Ovarian Function Trial):** randomized premenopausal HR+ women to:
  - [[tamoxifen]] alone
  - OFS + tamoxifen
  - OFS + exemestane (an [[aromatase-inhibitor]])
- **TEXT (Tamoxifen and EXemestane Trial):** randomized premenopausal HR+ women (all of whom received OFS) to:
  - OFS + tamoxifen
  - OFS + exemestane
- **Combined SOFT/TEXT analysis: OFS + exemestane significantly reduced recurrence** vs OFS + tamoxifen in **high-risk** premenopausal HR+ disease — typically defined by features such as younger age, higher grade, larger tumor size, or node positivity [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Implications by risk strata:**
  - **High-risk premenopausal:** OFS + AI is preferred per SOFT/TEXT
  - **Lower-risk premenopausal:** tamoxifen alone (with or without OFS) is acceptable; OFS + tamoxifen is also reasonable
- **Where this lands in the Q4 decision:** the Q4 patient is high-risk (Grade 3, 2.5 cm, RS 22, age 39 with intermediate genomic risk + adverse clinical features). If she chooses **non-chemotherapy adjuvant therapy**, SOFT/TEXT support **OFS + AI** as the regimen of choice. See [[intermediate-rs-premenopausal-hr-positive-management]].
- **The crucial caveat for Q4:** SOFT/TEXT compared **endocrine regimens to each other** — they did **not** test endocrine therapy vs chemotherapy. The "OFS+AI vs chemo+endocrine" question is what [[ofset-trial]] is designed to answer.
- **Toxicity:** OFS+AI carries more menopausal symptom burden, more bone-density loss, and more arthralgia than tamoxifen-based regimens. Quality-of-life trade-offs are real and matter to patient choice.
- **Long-term follow-up:** sustained recurrence reduction with OFS+AI in higher-risk premenopausal disease across multiple updates.
