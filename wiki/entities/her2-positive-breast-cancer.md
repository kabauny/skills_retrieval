# HER2-positive breast cancer

## Overview

Breast cancer subtype defined by overexpression or amplification of the human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (ERBB2/HER2). Comprises ~15–20% of breast cancers historically, now further stratified by hormone receptor (HR) status into HR+/HER2+ and HR−/HER2+, which behave differently and may warrant different sequencing decisions. **HR+/HER2+** is the subtype relevant to the Q3 scenario.

## Key facts

- **Standard early-stage treatment paradigm:** neoadjuvant chemotherapy + dual HER2 blockade (trastuzumab + pertuzumab), then surgery, then adjuvant therapy stratified by pathologic response [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **TCHP regimen** (taxane + carboplatin + trastuzumab + pertuzumab) is the most common neoadjuvant backbone for higher-stage HER2+ disease [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **Adjuvant therapy by pathologic response:**
  - **pCR** → continue trastuzumab (+ pertuzumab in node-positive at presentation) to complete one year of HER2 blockade.
  - **Residual invasive disease (non-pCR)** → switch to [[trastuzumab-emtansine]] per [[katherine-trial]] — current standard. Likely to shift to [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]] per [[destiny-breast05]] after expected July 2026 FDA action [[mrd-her2-breast-cancer-residual-disease-2026]].
- **HR+/HER2+ subset:** receives endocrine therapy in parallel with HER2-directed adjuvant therapy. The Q3 scenario specifically concerns HR+/HER2+ residual-disease patients with additional ctDNA MRD+ enrichment — the highest-risk subset of an already high-risk group. See [[residual-disease-vs-mrd-positivity]].
- **Brain metastasis risk** is elevated in HER2+ disease, motivating preference for CNS-active agents like [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]] (active in CNS per [[destiny-breast05]]) and [[tucatinib]] (in metastatic settings).
