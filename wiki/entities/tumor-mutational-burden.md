# Tumor mutational burden (TMB)

## Overview

The number of somatic mutations per megabase of tumor DNA. **High TMB** generates more neoantigens, providing more substrate for T-cell-mediated tumor recognition — the rationale for TMB as a predictive biomarker for **immune checkpoint inhibitor** response. In TNBC, TMB has shown prognostic value in [[keynote-522]] but **does not yet guide treatment de-escalation**.

## Key facts

- **TNBC context ([[keynote-522]] exploratory analysis, December 2024):** TMB was positively prognostic for improved pCR and EFS, with stronger correlation with EFS in the [[pembrolizumab]]-treated arm. Pattern consistent with TMB enriching for IO-responsive tumors [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **However:** [[pembrolizumab]] benefit was observed across all subgroups regardless of TMB status — meaning **TMB does not select patients for treatment** in untreated TNBC. The KEYNOTE-522 regimen is recommended for all eligible high-risk early TNBC, with no biomarker-guided de-escalation [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Companion biomarker mentioned in the same analysis:** T-cell inflamed 18-gene expression profile (TcellinfGEP) — also prognostic but similarly not actionable for de-escalation in untreated patients.
- **Pan-tumor TMB-high indication:** pembrolizumab is FDA-approved for TMB-high (≥10 mut/Mb) solid tumors that have progressed on prior therapy — a different setting from TNBC adjuvant use.
