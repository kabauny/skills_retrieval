# Ovarian function suppression (OFS)

## Overview

Pharmacologic suppression of ovarian estrogen production in premenopausal women, typically using **GnRH agonists** (goserelin, leuprolide, triptorelin) given monthly or every 3 months. Used as adjuvant treatment in HR+ premenopausal breast cancer, almost always in combination with either [[tamoxifen]] or an [[aromatase-inhibitor]]. AIs require concurrent OFS in premenopausal women — they only work in a postmenopausal hormonal milieu, so OFS is what creates that milieu.

## Key facts

- **Mechanism:** continuous GnRH-receptor agonism causes pituitary desensitization → suppressed LH/FSH → suppressed ovarian estradiol production → effectively "chemical menopause" while on therapy [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Reversibility:** OFS is generally reversible after discontinuation (relevant for fertility planning) — unlike surgical oophorectomy.
- **Pivotal evidence base — [[soft-text-trials|SOFT and TEXT trials]]:** OFS + exemestane (an AI) **significantly reduced recurrence** vs OFS + tamoxifen in high-risk premenopausal HR+ disease [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]]. Together SOFT/TEXT establish OFS+AI as the strongest non-chemotherapy adjuvant option in premenopausal HR+ disease.
- **OFS+AI vs OFS+tamoxifen:** OFS+AI is preferred for higher-risk patients; OFS+tamoxifen acceptable for lower-risk. AI without OFS is **not** appropriate in premenopausal women.
- **NCCN 2025–2026 framing:** for premenopausal HR+ disease, adjuvant endocrine therapy options include tamoxifen (with or without OFS) or an AI (with OFS) for 5 years [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Q4 relevance — the central role:** the alternative to chemotherapy in the Q4 decision is **OFS + AI** (per [[soft-text-trials|SOFT/TEXT]]), the strongest non-chemo regimen available for a premenopausal high-risk patient. Whether this is sufficient on its own — or whether chemotherapy adds something beyond ovarian suppression — is the question [[ofset-trial]] is designed to answer.
- **Toxicity profile:** menopausal symptoms (hot flashes, vaginal dryness, mood changes), bone-density loss (long-term), and quality-of-life impact in young women. Bone-density monitoring + bisphosphonates often indicated.
