# Tamoxifen

## Overview

Selective estrogen receptor modulator (SERM): antagonist in breast tissue (anti-tumor effect), partial agonist in bone (bone-density preservation) and endometrium (small endometrial cancer risk). The historical anchor of premenopausal HR+ adjuvant endocrine therapy and still in active use, especially in lower-risk premenopausal patients or where OFS is not desired.

## Key facts

- **Mechanism:** competitive ER antagonism in breast tissue. Active in both pre- and postmenopausal women — does not require ovarian status manipulation, unlike [[aromatase-inhibitor]] use.
- **Use as monotherapy:** standard 5-year (or extended 10-year) adjuvant tamoxifen is an option for premenopausal HR+ patients, particularly those with lower-risk disease or who decline/cannot tolerate [[ovarian-function-suppression]].
- **Use with [[ovarian-function-suppression]]:** OFS + tamoxifen is an alternative regimen to OFS + AI. **OFS + AI was superior in [[soft-text-trials|SOFT/TEXT]] for higher-risk patients**, so OFS + tamoxifen tends to be reserved for lower-risk patients within the OFS-eligible group [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Toxicity profile:**
  - Hot flashes, mood changes, vaginal dryness or discharge
  - **Endometrial cancer risk** (small absolute increase, requires monitoring of abnormal uterine bleeding)
  - **Thromboembolic risk** (DVT, PE) — especially in older patients or those with other risk factors
  - Cataracts (small risk)
- **Drug interactions:** tamoxifen is activated by CYP2D6. Strong CYP2D6 inhibitors (fluoxetine, paroxetine) may reduce efficacy — usually relevant when SSRIs are co-prescribed for hot flashes; consider venlafaxine instead.
- **Q4 relevance:** in the Q4 patient choosing **non-chemotherapy** adjuvant therapy, the practical options are:
  - **OFS + AI** (preferred for high-risk per SOFT/TEXT) — see [[aromatase-inhibitor]]
  - **OFS + tamoxifen** — acceptable alternative
  - **Tamoxifen alone** — typically lower-risk option; less appropriate given the Q4 patient's high-risk clinical features (Grade 3, 2.5 cm)
