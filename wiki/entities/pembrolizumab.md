# Pembrolizumab

## Overview

Humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody targeting **PD-1** on T cells. Blocks PD-1/PD-L1 ligation, releasing T-cell exhaustion and restoring anti-tumor immune response. Approved across many tumor types; in breast cancer, the principal indication is high-risk early-stage [[triple-negative-breast-cancer]] in combination with chemotherapy (the [[keynote-522]] regimen).

## Key facts

- **Mechanism:** PD-1 antagonism on tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes → restores T-cell effector function in the tumor microenvironment. Class effect shared with nivolumab and other anti-PD-1 agents.
- **TNBC indication ([[keynote-522]]):**
  - Neoadjuvant: pembrolizumab + chemotherapy (carboplatin + paclitaxel → AC) × 8 cycles
  - Adjuvant: pembrolizumab × 9 cycles (~1 year), regardless of pCR
  - Standard dose: 200 mg IV q3w or 400 mg IV q6w
- **5-year OS benefit ([[keynote-522]]):** 86.6% vs 81.7% (HR 0.66, P=0.0015), confirmed at ESMO 2024 [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **pCR subgroup OS benefit is small:** 95.1% vs 94.4% — most of the regimen's overall OS gain is in non-pCR patients. **Directly relevant to the Q5 calculus** [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Toxicity profile:**
  - **Grade ≥3 [[immune-related-adverse-events]] in 13.0%** of patients (KEYNOTE-522), vs 1.5% with placebo [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - GI events (colitis) most common Grade ≥3 irAE — ~32% of severe events
  - Adjuvant-phase irAE rate: 18.1% in real-world data [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - Endocrine irAEs (hypo/hyperthyroidism) often irreversible but manageable with hormone replacement
  - Rare fatal events: adrenal crisis, autoimmune encephalitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, sepsis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
- **Re-challenge after irAE:** 53% successfully re-challenged in real-world data; 28.8% irAE recurrence (~23% specifically for pneumonitis/colitis) [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]. See [[pembrolizumab-rechallenge-after-severe-irae]].

## Beyond TNBC (passing references in current sources)

Active across many other tumor types — NSCLC, melanoma, MSI-H tumors, and others. Future ingests in those areas will accrete onto this entity page. For now, only the TNBC context is wiki-citable.
