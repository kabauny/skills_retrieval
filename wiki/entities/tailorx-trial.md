# TAILORx trial

## Overview

Phase 3 trial that established [[oncotype-dx-recurrence-score]] as the primary genomic decision tool in HR+/HER2− **node-negative** early breast cancer. Showed that most women with intermediate Recurrence Score (RS 11–25) can safely forgo adjuvant chemotherapy with endocrine therapy alone — **except** for a specific premenopausal high-risk subgroup that does derive a small but real benefit. The Q4 patient lives in that exception.

## Key facts

- **Population:** HR+, HER2−, **node-negative** early breast cancer with Oncotype DX RS available [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Design:** randomized assignment by RS:
  - RS ≤10: endocrine therapy alone (low-risk arm)
  - RS 11–25: **randomized** to endocrine therapy alone vs. chemo-endocrine therapy
  - RS ≥26: chemo-endocrine therapy (high-risk arm)
- **Primary result (RS 11–25 randomized arm):** non-inferiority of endocrine therapy alone — most women with intermediate RS can skip chemotherapy [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Critical premenopausal subgroup analysis:** women aged ≤50 with **RS 16–25**, especially with high-risk clinical features (Grade 3, larger tumor, younger age), **did derive a small but significant chemotherapy benefit**. This finding has persisted at **12-year follow-up** [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **April 2024 NCDB retrospective:** OS benefit for chemotherapy in <50yo node-negative women with intermediate RS, **especially RS 21–25** [[premenopausal-intermediate-oncotype-2026]].
- **Clinical implication for the Q4 case (39yo, RS 22, Grade 3, 2.5 cm, N0):** TAILORx is the directly relevant trial. The patient sits in the premenopausal intermediate-RS subgroup with high-risk clinical features where chemotherapy benefit was demonstrated.
- **Distinct from [[rxponder-trial]]:** RxPONDER addressed node-positive (1–3 nodes) disease, not node-negative. The two trials answer different questions for different populations. See [[node-negative-vs-node-positive-genomic-trial-scoping]].
