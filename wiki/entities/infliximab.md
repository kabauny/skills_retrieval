# Infliximab

## Overview

Chimeric monoclonal antibody targeting tumor necrosis factor alpha (TNF-α). Originally developed for inflammatory bowel disease and rheumatologic conditions. In oncology, used as **second-line agent for steroid-refractory immune-related colitis** during checkpoint-inhibitor therapy. The Q5 patient received infliximab for Grade 3 colitis that did not respond to high-dose corticosteroids.

## Key facts

- **Mechanism:** binds and neutralizes soluble and membrane-bound TNF-α, blunting the inflammatory cascade driving immune-mediated colitis.
- **Use in [[immune-related-adverse-events]]:** added when high-dose corticosteroid therapy (prednisone 1–2 mg/kg/day or IV methylprednisolone) fails to improve Grade ≥3 immune-mediated colitis within 2–3 days [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]].
- **Typical dosing for irAE colitis:** single dose 5 mg/kg IV; can be repeated. Steroid taper continues over 4–6 weeks alongside.
- **Implication of needing infliximab:**
  - Marker of severe, steroid-refractory colitis — the Q5 patient's clinical course
  - Increases the **risk-magnitude** of any future re-challenge with [[pembrolizumab]] — 23% irAE recurrence specifically for colitis [[tnbc-keynote-522-irae-rechallenge-2026]]
  - Should weigh into the [[pembrolizumab-rechallenge-after-severe-irae]] decision
- **Infectious caveats:** check TB status and hepatitis B serology before infliximab in non-emergent settings; most irAE infliximab use is urgent so the infectious workup happens in parallel.
