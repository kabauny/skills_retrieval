### [2026-04-26] Q5 TNBC pCR + Grade 3 colitis — adjuvant pembrolizumab omission

- **Scenario:** High-risk early-stage [[triple-negative-breast-cancer]] patient achieves pCR after neoadjuvant [[keynote-522]] regimen but developed **Grade 3 immune-mediated colitis** during neoadjuvant phase requiring **steroids + [[infliximab]]**. Generic case framing (no specified comorbidities, autoimmune history, or fertility plans).
- **Options considered:**
  - **A.** Omit adjuvant [[pembrolizumab]]
  - **B.** Complete the full 9-cycle adjuvant year (re-challenge)
  - **C.** Truncated adjuvant (e.g., 2–3 cycles instead of 9; no evidence base)
- **Decision:** **Option A — omit adjuvant pembrolizumab.**
- **Reasoning (verbatim):** "If a patient has achieved pCR, additional immunotherapy is not needed. The risk does not outweigh the benefits."
- **Reasoning (tight summary):** pCR is itself an excellent-prognosis marker; the ~0.7% absolute marginal OS benefit in the pCR subgroup does not justify the ~23% colitis-recurrence risk on re-challenge after a Grade 3 irAE that already required infliximab (i.e., steroid-refractory severity).
- **Evidence cited:** [[keynote-522]] (pCR-subgroup 5-yr OS 95.1% vs 94.4%); [[pembrolizumab-rechallenge-after-severe-irae]] (~23% colitis recurrence on re-challenge; Grade 3 prior is outside the typical re-challenge candidate profile); [[adjuvant-pembrolizumab-after-pcr-tnbc]] (decision skeleton; "road to pCR may matter" framing); [[immune-related-adverse-events]] (severity grading context); [[infliximab]] (marker of steroid-refractory severity)
- **Confidence:** moderate
- **Wiki tension noted:** Deviates from formal NCCN/ASCO/ESMO recommendation (full regimen regardless of pCR). Deviation falls in a **guideline-gap area** — irAE-driven discontinuation in pCR patients is not specifically addressed by current guidelines.
