# Triple-negative breast cancer pathologic complete response after neoadjuvant KEYNOTE-522 regimen — adjuvant pembrolizumab continuation vs omission after severe immune-related toxicity, 2025-2026

The KEYNOTE-522 regimen, combining neoadjuvant pembrolizumab with chemotherapy followed by adjuvant pembrolizumab, has become a standard of care for high-risk, early-stage triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC), demonstrating significant improvements in pathologic complete response (pCR), event-free survival (EFS), and overall survival (OS). Recent updates in 2025-2026 further solidify its role and provide insights into various aspects of patient management.

Here's an overview focusing on the requested points:

### 1. Prognostic Value of pCR in TNBC after KEYNOTE-522 Regimen

Pathologic complete response (pCR) remains a strong prognostic indicator in TNBC after the KEYNOTE-522 regimen. The trial showed that the addition of pembrolizumab significantly increased pCR rates to 64.8% compared to 51.2% with chemotherapy alone [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/updated-data-further-support-keynote-522-regimen-as-soc-in-high-risk-early-stage-tnbc) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/october-25-2024/overall-survival-analysis-confirms-pembrolizumab-regimen-as-standard-of-care-for-triple-negative-breast-cancer/) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11843215/) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11461162/). Patients achieving pCR generally experience improved event-free survival (EFS) and overall survival (OS) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11843215/). Notably, the highest OS benefit was observed in patients with the least residual cancer burden (RCB-0), indicating that a complete pathological response is associated with the best long-term outcomes [cancernetwork.com](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/46-neoadjuvant-pembrolizumab-or-placebo-plus-chemotherapy-followed-by-adjuvant-pembrolizumab-or-placebo-for-high-risk-early-stage-triple-negative-breast-cancer-overall-survival-and-subgroup-results-from-the-phase-3-keynote-522-study). Conversely, pCR was found to be less likely in patients with cN3 nodal status and ECOG performance status of 1 [ascopubs.org](https://ascopubs.org/doi/10.1200/JCO.2025.43.16_suppl.e13153).

Exploratory biomarker analyses from KEYNOTE-522, presented as recently as December 2024, indicate that biomarkers such as the T-cell inflamed 18-gene expression profile (TcellinfGEP) and tumor mutational burden (TMB) were positively prognostic for improved pCR and EFS [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/tmb-and-gene-profiles-confirm-prognostic-value-in-tnbc) [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/breast-cancer/biomarkers-linked-to-pathologic-response-in-early-triple-negative-breast-cancer). TMB specifically correlated with improved EFS in the pembrolizumab-treated arm, suggesting that these biomarkers might reflect an enhanced immune response, although pembrolizumab demonstrated efficacy regardless of these markers in the overall population [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/tmb-and-gene-profiles-confirm-prognostic-value-in-tnbc).

### 2. Adjuvant Pembrolizumab Continuation vs. Omission After pCR

The KEYNOTE-522 trial design included the continuation of adjuvant pembrolizumab for up to nine cycles (approximately one year) regardless of whether a pCR was achieved [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/updated-data-further-support-keynote-522-regimen-as-soc-in-high-risk-early-stage-tnbc) [ascopubs.org](https://dailynews.ascopubs.org/do/adjuvant-therapy-residual-disease-early-stage-triple-negative-breast-cancer) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/february-10-2020/neoadjuvant-and-adjuvant-pembrolizumab-improves-pcr-rate-in-tnbc/) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11461162/). Updated overall survival data, presented at ESMO 2024 and published in October 2024, confirm a significant OS benefit with the full neoadjuvant and adjuvant pembrolizumab regimen (5-year OS: 86.6% with pembrolizumab vs 81.7% with placebo; HR 0.66; P = .0015) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/october-25-2024/overall-survival-analysis-confirms-pembrolizumab-regimen-as-standard-of-care-for-triple-negative-breast-cancer/) [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/article/practice-changing-data-in-triple-negative-breast-cancer). This OS benefit was observed in both patients who achieved pCR and those who did not, suggesting that the "road to pCR may matter" [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/article/practice-changing-data-in-triple-negative-breast-cancer) [nccn.org](https://education.nccn.org/sites/default/files/course/2024-02/05_Santa-Maria_NCCNbc24_v1.pdf). For patients achieving pCR, the 5-year OS rate was 95.1% in the pembrolizumab arm versus 94.4% in the placebo arm, indicating a sustained, albeit numerically smaller, benefit even in this favorable group [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/october-25-2024/overall-survival-analysis-confirms-pembrolizumab-regimen-as-standard-of-care-for-triple-negative-breast-cancer/).

The question of whether the full year of adjuvant pembrolizumab is strictly necessary in patients who achieve pCR, particularly in terms of whether the immune-oncology (IO) benefit is primarily neoadjuvant or also adjuvant, remains an area of active discussion. While the trial was not designed to isolate the adjuvant benefit after pCR, the overall favorable outcomes with the combined neoadjuvant and adjuvant approach led to its approval and guideline recommendations [ascopubs.org](https://dailynews.ascopubs.org/do/adjuvant-therapy-residual-disease-early-stage-triple-negative-breast-cancer) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11461162/) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/may-25-2022/asco-guideline-rapid-update-addresses-the-use-of-neoadjuvant-pembrolizumab-in-high-risk-early-stage-triple-negative-breast-cancer/) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/new-guidelines-move-beyond-chemotherapy-for-patients-with-triple-negative-breast-cancer) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11094404/). However, real-world data from April 2025 indicated that 18.1% of patients experienced immune-related adverse events (irAEs) during the adjuvant pembrolizumab phase, which raises concerns about its continued use, especially in patients who have already achieved pCR [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12496205/).

### 3. Management of Severe (Grade 3+) Immune-Related Adverse Events

Severe (Grade 3+) immune-related adverse events (irAEs) can occur with pembrolizumab. In the KEYNOTE-522 trial, Grade 3 or higher immune-mediated AEs occurred in 13.0% of patients receiving pembrolizumab plus chemotherapy, compared to 1.5% in the placebo group [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/article/practice-changing-data-in-triple-negative-breast-cancer) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/updated-guidelines-recommend-adding-pembrolizumab-to-neoadjuvant-chemotherapy-in-tnbc). Real-world data show a similar incidence, with 13.6% of patients experiencing Grade ≥3 irAEs, with gastrointestinal events being the most predominant (32% of Grade ≥3 irAEs).

Immune checkpoint inhibitors should be withheld, and systemic corticosteroid therapy (e.g., high-dose prednisone 1-2 mg/kg/day or intravenous methylprednisolone) should be initiated.
