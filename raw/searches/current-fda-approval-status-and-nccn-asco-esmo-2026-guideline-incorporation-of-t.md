# Current FDA approval status and NCCN ASCO ESMO 2026 guideline incorporation of trastuzumab deruxtecan T-DXd for adjuvant HER2-positive residual breast cancer after DESTINY-Breast05 — April 2026 update

Here's an April 2026 update on the FDA approval status and guideline incorporation of trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) for adjuvant HER2-positive residual breast cancer after DESTINY-Breast05, focusing on changes since late 2025/early 2026:

**1. FDA Approval Status of T-DXd for Adjuvant HER2+ Residual Disease Post-Neoadjuvant**
As of April 2026, T-DXd (Enhertu) has **not yet received FDA approval** for the adjuvant treatment of HER2-positive early breast cancer with residual invasive disease after neoadjuvant therapy. However, its supplemental Biologics License Application (sBLA) was accepted and granted Priority Review by the U.S. FDA on March 9, 2026. [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/documents/36782/0/ENHERTU_DESTINY-Breast05_Priority+Review_Press+Release_FINAL_03.09.26.pdf/481e500e-0745-c779-10bf-6a40af8587b7?t=1772988909600) [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/press-releases/-/article/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer) [curetoday.com](https://www.curetoday.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-enhertu-for-her2-positive-breast-cancer) [astrazeneca.com](https://www.astrazeneca.com/media-centre/press-releases/2026/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer.html) This Priority Review follows the Breakthrough Therapy Designation granted by the FDA in December 2025. [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/documents/36782/0/ENHERTU_DESTINY-Breast05_Priority+Review_Press+Release_FINAL_03.09.26.pdf/481e500e-0745-c779-10bf-6a40af8587b7?t=1772988909600) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer) [cancerletter.com](https://cancerletter.com/drugs-and-targets/20260109_10b/) The Prescription Drug User Fee Act (PDUFA) target action date for the FDA's regulatory decision is set for **July 7, 2026**. [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/documents/36782/0/ENHERTU_DESTINY-Breast05_Priority+Review_Press+Release_FINAL_03.09.26.pdf/481e500e-0745-c779-10bf-6a40af8587b7?t=1772988909600) [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/press-releases/-/article/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer)

The application is supported by data from the DESTINY-Breast05 phase 3 trial, which demonstrated that T-DXd significantly reduced the risk of invasive disease recurrence or death (invasive disease-free survival [IDFS]) by 53% compared to trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1) in this patient population. [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/documents/36782/0/ENHERTU_DESTINY-Breast05_Priority+Review_Press+Release_FINAL_03.09.26.pdf/481e500e-0745-c779-10bf-6a40af8587b7?t=1772988909600) [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/press-releases/-/article/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer) The three-year IDFS rate was 92.4% in the T-DXd arm versus 83.7% with T-DM1. [daiichisankyo.us](https://daiichisankyo.us/documents/36782/0/ENHERTU_DESTINY-Breast05_Priority+Review_Press+Release_FINAL_03.09.26.pdf/481e500e-0745-c779-10bf-6a40af8587b7?t=1772988909600) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer)

**2. NCCN, ASCO, or ESMO Guideline Incorporation**
*   **NCCN:** As of April 2026, the available information from NCCN guidelines, including "NCCN Guidelines for Breast Cancer Version 1.2026" and "NCCN Guidelines for Patients: Metastatic Breast Cancer, 2025," does not explicitly show the incorporation of T-DXd as a preferred adjuvant therapy for HER2-positive residual disease post-neoadjuvant. [nccn.org](https://www.nccn.org/patients/guidelines/content/PDF/stage_iv_breast-patient.pdf) [scribd.com](https://www.scribd.com/document/1006711870/Breast-Cancer-Nccn-2026) [nccn.org](https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/guidelines-detail?category=1&id=1419) NCCN updates throughout 2025 primarily focused on metastatic settings or other breast cancer subtypes, or general updates to guidelines structure. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/experts-unpack-the-most-notable-nccn-guideline-changes-heading-into-2026)
*   **ASCO:** ASCO has listed "UPDATE: Medical Therapy for Patients with Stage I-III HER2-Positive Breast Cancer" as a guideline development priority for 2025-2026, indicating that updates incorporating new data, such as from DESTINY-Breast05, are anticipated or in progress. [asco.org](https://www.asco.org/practice-patients/guidelines/topic-priorities) While some experts, based on DESTINY-Breast05 data, have stated that adjuvant T-DXd "should be adopted as the standard of care" for high-risk patients, an official, updated ASCO guideline document for this specific adjuvant indication has not been published as of April 2026. [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/november-10-2025/her2-positive-early-breast-cancer-trastuzumab-deruxtecan-moves-into-earlier-clinical-settings/)
*   **ESMO:** Data from DESTINY-Breast05, presented at the ESMO Congress 2025 (October 2025), led to expert opinion that T-DXd "should replace T-DM1 as the new SOC for patients with HER2-positive, residual invasive breast cancer after neoadjuvant therapy." [esmo.org](https://dailyreporter.esmo.org/esmo-congress-2025/breast-cancer/trastuzumab-deruxtecan-is-moving-to-the-curative-setting-in-early-her2-positive-breast-cancer) However, the latest general ESMO guidelines for metastatic breast cancer (v1.2 - April 2025) still refer to T-DXd for metastatic or HER2-low disease, not yet specifically for adjuvant HER2-positive residual disease after neoadjuvant therapy. [esmo.org](https://www.esmo.org/guidelines/living-guidelines/esmo-living-guideline-metastatic-breast-cancer/hr-positive-her2-negative-metastatic-breast-cancer/hr-her2-de-novo-mbc-or-recurrence-12-months-after-the-end-of-adjuvant-et-et-sensitive-naive/hr-her2-mbc-progressive-disease-not-candidate-for-endocrine-therapy-targeted-therapy/antibody-drug-conjugates) This suggests that while there is strong scientific and expert consensus, official guideline updates may still be pending formal publication.

**3. Newer or Longer Follow-up Data on DESTINY-Breast05**
Recent updates from late 2025 and early 2026 primarily reiterate the initial impressive results presented at ESMO 2025 and subsequently published in The New England Journal of Medicine.
*   **Overall Survival (OS) Data:** As of April 2026, further mature overall survival data from DESTINY-Breast05 beyond the initial invasive disease-free survival (IDFS) results have not been specifically reported. OS remains a secondary endpoint, and the data are likely still maturing. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer) [astrazeneca.com](https://www.astrazeneca.com/media-centre/press-releases/2026/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer.html)
*   **Brain Metastasis Subgroup:** T-DXd significantly lowered the risk of brain metastases (brain metastasis-free interval) by 36% compared with T-DM1 (HR 0.64; 95% CI 0.35-1.17). [curetoday.com](https://www.curetoday.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-enhertu-for-her2-positive-breast-cancer) [astrazeneca.com](https://www.astrazeneca.com/media-centre/press-releases/2026/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer.html) This finding was highlighted in March 2026 press releases. [curetoday.com](https://www.curetoday.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-enhertu-for-her2-positive-breast-cancer) [astrazeneca.com](https://www.astrazeneca.com/media-centre/press-releases/2026/enhertu-granted-priority-review-in-the-us-as-post-neoadjuvant-treatment-for-patients-with-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer.html) Experts noted that T-DXd has greater activity in the brain compared to T-DM1, which is "extremely important because brain metastases are associated with a dismal prognosis." [esmo.org](https://dailyreporter.esmo.org/esmo-congress-2025/breast-cancer/trastuzumab-deruxtecan-is-moving-to-the-curative-setting-in-early-her2-positive-breast-cancer)
*   **ILD Safety Updates:** The safety profile of T-DXd observed in DESTINY-Breast05 was consistent with its known profile, with no new safety concerns identified. Rates of interstitial lung disease (ILD) were 9.6% in the T-DXd arm (including two Grade 5 events) versus 1.6% in the T-DM1 arm (no Grade 5 events). [esmo.org](https://dailyreporter.esmo.org/esmo-congress-2025/breast-cancer/trastuzumab-deruxtecan-is-moving-to-the-curative-setting-in-early-her2-positive-breast-cancer) [astrazeneca.com](https://www.astrazeneca.com/media-centre/press-releases/2025/enhertu-reduced-the-risk-of-disease-recurrence-or-death-by-53-vs-t-dm1-in-patients-with-high-risk-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer.html) [oncoprescribe.com](https://www.oncoprescribe.com/destiny-breast05-a-new-direction-in-postneoadjuvant-care-for-high-risk-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer/) Proactive ILD monitoring, including serial low-dose chest CT imaging, was incorporated into the trial, enabling early detection. No increased ILD risk was observed with concurrent radiotherapy. [oncoprescribe.com](https://www.oncoprescribe.com/destiny-breast05-a-new-direction-in-postneoadjuvant-care-for-high-risk-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer/)

**4. KATHERINE 10-year Long-term Follow-up**
An updated long-term analysis of the Phase III KATHERINE trial was published in January 2025, with a median follow-up of 8.4 years. [appliedclinicaltrialsonline.com](https://www.appliedclinicaltrialsonline.com/view/katherine-trial-kadcyla-survival-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/news/january-2025/adjuvant-t-dm1-in-patients-with-her2-positive-breast-cancer-long-term-results-of-the-katherine-trial/) This analysis confirmed that adjuvant T-DM1 significantly improved overall survival and long-term invasive disease-free survival compared to trastuzumab monotherapy in patients with HER2-positive early breast cancer with residual invasive disease. [appliedclinicaltrialsonline.com](https://www.appliedclinicaltrialsonline.com/view/katherine-trial-kadcyla-survival-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/news/january-2025/adjuvant-t-dm1-in-patients-with-her2-positive-breast-cancer-long-term-results-of-the-katherine-trial/) Specifically, the 7-year IDFS rate was 80.8% with T-DM1 versus 67.1% with trastuzumab monotherapy, and the 7-year OS rate was 89.1% versus 84.4%, respectively. [appliedclinicaltrialsonline.com](https://www.appliedclinicaltrialsonline.com/view/katherine-trial-kadcyla-survival-her2-positive-early-breast-cancer) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/news/january-2025/adjuvant-t-dm1-in-patients-with-her2-positive-breast-cancer-long-term-results-of-the-katherine-trial/) This report represents the most recent long-term data available for the KATHERINE trial, without explicitly stating a "10-year" follow-up.
