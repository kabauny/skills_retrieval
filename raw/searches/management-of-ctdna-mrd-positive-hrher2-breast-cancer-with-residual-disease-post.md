# Management of ctDNA MRD-positive HR+/HER2+ breast cancer with residual disease post-neoadjuvant TCHP — adjuvant T-DM1 vs T-DXd vs adding tucatinib, 2025-2026 evidence and guidelines

The management of ctDNA MRD-positive HR+/HER2+ breast cancer with residual disease after neoadjuvant TCHP is an evolving area, with significant new evidence emerging in 2025-2026. The current standard of care is being challenged by highly effective novel agents, while MRD-guided therapy strategies are still primarily investigational.

### Current Adjuvant Standard of Care: T-DM1 (Trastuzumab Emtansine)

For patients with HER2-positive early breast cancer who have residual invasive disease following neoadjuvant trastuzumab-based chemotherapy (including TCHP), adjuvant trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1) is the established standard of care. This recommendation is based on the pivotal KATHERINE trial, which demonstrated a significant improvement in invasive disease-free survival (IDFS) with T-DM1 compared to trastuzumab alone. [medscape.com](https://decisionpoint.medscape.com/oncology/viewarticle/918066) [cancernetwork.com](https://www.cancernetwork.com/view/subgroup-analyses-of-katherine-trial-promote-t-dm1-use-in-high-risk-groups) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/december-25-2018/katherine-trial/) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/exploratory-analyses-of-katherine-trial-inform-adjuvant-t-dm1-use-in-her2-breast-cancer) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7724193/) [nccn.org](https://education.nccn.org/sites/default/files/course/2024-02/03_Robinson_NCCNbc24_v3.pdf) [2minutemedicine.com](https://www.2minutemedicine.com/trastuzumab-emtansine-improves-outcomes-in-residual-her2-positive-breast-cancer/) [providence.org](https://digitalcommons.providence.org/publications/5216/) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/asco-s-early-breast-cancer-guidelines-updated-to-include-t-dm1)

*   **Prospective Trial Data (KATHERINE):** The KATHERINE trial randomized patients with residual invasive disease to receive either T-DM1 or trastuzumab for 14 cycles. T-DM1 reduced the risk of an invasive disease-free survival event at 3 years by 50% (IDFS 88.3% vs 77.0%). [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/december-25-2018/katherine-trial/) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/exploratory-analyses-of-katherine-trial-inform-adjuvant-t-dm1-use-in-her2-breast-cancer) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7724193/) This benefit was consistently observed across various subgroups, including patients with hormone receptor-positive disease. [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/december-25-2018/katherine-trial/) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7724193/) Long-term follow-up (8.4 years) confirmed sustained benefits in IDFS and showed improved overall survival with T-DM1. [nccn.org](https://education.nccn.org/sites/default/files/course/2024-02/03_Robinson_NCCNbc24_v3.pdf) [2minutemedicine.com](https://www.2minutemedicine.com/trastuzumab-emtansine-improves-outcomes-in-residual-her2-positive-breast-cancer/)
*   **On-label Status:** T-DM1 is FDA-approved for this indication and is a Category 1 recommendation in NCCN guidelines for HER2-positive residual breast cancer. [medscape.com](https://decisionpoint.medscape.com/oncology/viewarticle/918066) [nccn.org](https://education.nccn.org/sites/default/files/course/2024-02/03_Robinson_NCCNbc24_v3.pdf) [ascopubs.org](https://ascopubs.org/doi/10.1200/OP.23.00563) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/asco-s-early-breast-cancer-guidelines-updated-to-include-t-dm1) ASCO guidelines also support its use. [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/asco-s-early-breast-cancer-guidelines-updated-to-include-t-dm1)

### Emerging Adjuvant Standard: T-DXd (Trastuzumab Deruxtecan)

Trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) is rapidly emerging as a new standard of care, with compelling prospective trial data reported in late 2025.

*   **Prospective Trial Data (DESTINY-Breast05):** Positive topline results from the Phase 3 DESTINY-Breast05 trial, presented at ESMO 2025 and SABCS 2025, demonstrated a highly statistically significant and clinically meaningful improvement in IDFS for T-DXd compared to T-DM1 in patients with high-risk HER2-positive early breast cancer with residual invasive disease after neoadjuvant treatment. ### Tucatinib

Tucatinib is a tyrosine kinase inhibitor primarily known for its efficacy in metastatic HER2-positive breast cancer, particularly in patients with brain metastases.

*   **Extrapolation from Metastatic Data:** Tucatinib, in combination with trastuzumab and capecitabine, is FDA-approved for advanced HER2-positive metastatic breast cancer based on the HER2CLIMB trial, which demonstrated improved overall survival, including in patients with brain metastases. [nih.gov](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40623091/) [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/breast-cancer/after-fda-approves-adc-combo-big-questions-remain-in-her2-positive-breast-cancer)
*   **Prospective Trial Data in Adjuvant Setting:** While tucatinib is being investigated in earlier-stage breast cancer, there are no definitive prospective trial results within the 2025-2026 timeframe directly comparing tucatinib (alone or added to other regimens) with T-DM1 or T-DXd in the adjuvant setting for MRD-positive HR+/HER2+ breast cancer with residual disease post-neoadjuvant TCHP. HER2CLIMB-05, presented at SABCS 2025, showed benefit when tucatinib was added to trastuzumab and pertuzumab as first-line *maintenance* therapy in *metastatic* HER2-positive breast cancer, demonstrating an 8.6-month improvement in progression-free survival. [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/march-25-2026/adding-tucatinib-to-first-line-maintenance-therapy-delays-disease-progression-in-her2-positive-metastatic-breast-cancer/) [pharmacytimes.com](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/tucatinib-enhances-first-line-maintenance-outcomes-in-her2-positive-metastatic-breast-cancer) [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/breast-cancer/after-fda-approves-adc-combo-big-questions-remain-in-her2-positive-breast-cancer)
*   **On-label/Investigational Status:** In the context of *adjuvant* therapy for MRD-positive HR+/HER2+ breast cancer with residual disease post-neoadjuvant TCHP, the use of tucatinib would currently be considered **investigational** or **off-label**, as its efficacy in this specific setting has not been established by dedicated adjuvant trials.

### MRD-Guided Therapy Escalation Strategies

The use of circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) for minimal residual disease (MRD) detection in early breast cancer is a rapidly developing field.

*   **Evidence:** Retrospective analyses and ongoing prospective studies indicate that ctDNA detection is highly prognostic for recurrence. [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12420329/) The presence of ctDNA in the absence of overt metastases defines molecular MRD and offers a potential window for intervention. [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12420329/)
*   **Prospective Trials:** Clinical trials are actively exploring the integration of ctDNA monitoring into patient management. For example, a trial (NCT07136493) is evaluating ctDNA-based MRD detection in early-stage breast cancer, including HER2-positive, to identify patients who might benefit from additional therapy and to associate ctDNA detectability with recurrence-free survival. [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07136493) However, a randomized Phase 3 trial (ZEST) designed to assess ctDNA surveillance to prevent recurrence closed early due to challenges in patient randomization, suggesting the need for more focused study designs in higher-risk populations. [healio.com](https://www.healio.com/news/hematology-oncology/20241213/terminated-studys-challenges-may-guide-future-trials-of-mrdguided-breast-cancer-therapy)
*   **Current Guidelines:** As of early 2026, NCCN, ASCO, and ESMO guidelines do not explicitly recommend routine ctDNA MRD testing to *guide escalation of adjuvant therapy* in HR+/HER2+ breast cancer. While ctDNA is recognized for its prognostic value, further prospective data demonstrating that intervening based on MRD positivity improves patient outcomes are needed before it becomes a standard of care for therapy escalation. [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12420329/) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/mrd-testing-presents-challenges-and-opportunities-in-breast-cancer-care) MRD clearance is being explored as a potential surrogate endpoint for ctDNA-guided adjuvant therapy. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/mrd-testing-presents-challenges-and-opportunities-in-breast-cancer-care)

### NCCN/ASCO/ESMO Guidance (2025-2026)

*   **T-DM1:** Remains the current guideline-recommended adjuvant therapy for HER2-positive patients with residual disease after neoadjuvant treatment. [medscape.com](https://decisionpoint.medscape.com/oncology/viewarticle/918066) [nccn.org](https://education.nccn.org/sites/default/files/course/2024-02/03_Robinson_NCCNbc24_v3.pdf) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/asco-s-early-breast-cancer-guidelines-updated-to-include-t-dm1)
*   **T-DXd:** Given the robust positive results from DESTINY-Breast05, T-DXd is widely anticipated to be incorporated into NCCN, ASCO, and ESMO guidelines as the new preferred adjuvant therapy for high-risk HER2-positive early breast cancer with residual disease following neoadjuvant therapy. While specific 2026 versions of all breast cancer guidelines are not yet fully available, the NCCN Invasive Breast Cancer - English Version 2025 is available [nccn.org](https://www.nccn.org/guidelines/guidelines-detail?category=1&id=1419), and updated guidelines from these organizations are expected to reflect these practice-changing data in the very near future. [daiichisankyo.com](https://www.daiichisankyo.com/files/news/pressrelease/pdf/202509/20250929_E.pdf) [vjoncology.com](https://www.vjoncology.com/video/r3c9kfnz1ee-destiny-breast05-t-dxd-vs-t-dm1-in-high-risk-her2-early-breast-cancer/) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/november-10-2025/her2-positive-early-breast-cancer-trastuzumab-deruxtecan-moves-into-earlier-clinical-settings/) [emjreviews.com](https://www.emjreviews.com/oncology/symposium/2025-round-up-human-epidermal-growth-factor-receptor-2-positive-early-breast-cancer-s33126/) [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/breast-cancer/which-data-in-2026-may-answer-big-questions-about-breast-cancer-treatment-after-t-dxd) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-grants-priority-review-to-post-neoadjuvant-t-dxd-for-her2-early-breast-cancer) [esmo.org](https://dailyreporter.esmo.org/esmo-congress-2025/breast-cancer/trastuzumab-deruxtecan-is-moving-to-the-curative-setting-in-early-her2-positive-breast-cancer)
*   **Tucatinib and MRD-Guided Therapy:** Current guidelines do not recommend tucatinib or ctDNA MRD-guided therapy escalation in the adjuvant setting. These approaches remain investigational for this specific context, awaiting further definitive prospective trial data.

In summary, for HR+/HER2+ breast cancer with residual disease post-neoadjuvant TCHP, T-DM1 is the current on-label and guideline-recommended adjuvant therapy. However, based on late 2025 data, T-DXd has demonstrated superior efficacy and is poised to become the new standard of care following anticipated regulatory approvals and guideline updates in mid-2026. Tucatinib remains an important agent in metastatic disease, but its role in the adjuvant MRD-positive setting is still investigational. MRD-guided therapy is a promising area of research, but not yet integrated into standard guidelines for adjuvant therapy escalation.
