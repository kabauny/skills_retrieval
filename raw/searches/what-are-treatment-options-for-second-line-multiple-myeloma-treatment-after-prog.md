# What are treatment options for second line multiple myeloma treatment after progressing on dara VRD?

When a patient progresses on frontline Dara-VRd (daratumumab, bortezomib, lenalidomide, and dexamethasone), they are generally considered refractory to an anti-CD38 monoclonal antibody, a first-generation proteasome inhibitor (PI), and an immunomodulatory drug (IMiD) [search-sourced]. Second-line treatment selection according to current clinical practice and recent National Comprehensive Cancer Network (NCCN) guidelines relies heavily on class switching, deploying next-generation agents, or utilizing novel B-cell maturation antigen (BCMA)-targeted therapies [search-sourced].

Key second-line options include:

**1. BCMA-Targeted CAR T-Cell Therapy**
*   **Ciltacabtagene autoleucel (Carvykti / cilta-cel):** In April 2024, the FDA expanded the approval of this personalized CAR T-cell therapy to the second-line setting [search-sourced]. It is approved for adult patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma who have received at least one prior line of therapy (which must include a PI and an IMiD) and who are refractory to lenalidomide [search-sourced]. The updated NCCN guidelines prioritize this as a highly effective option for eligible patients experiencing early relapse [search-sourced].

**2. Next-Generation PI and IMiD Regimens**
Because the patient has already progressed on bortezomib and lenalidomide, switching to second-generation agents from the same classes is a standard clinical strategy [search-sourced]. 
*   **Carfilzomib, pomalidomide, and dexamethasone (KPd):** This triplet regimen is strongly favored because it introduces both a second-generation PI (carfilzomib) and a second-generation IMiD (pomalidomide), circumventing immediate resistance to frontline agents [search-sourced].
*   **Carfilzomib and dexamethasone (Kd):** While doublet therapies are generally less preferred than triplets for relapsed disease, this remains a viable option to introduce a novel PI [search-sourced].

**3. BCMA-Targeted Bispecific Antibodies**
*   **Teclistamab (Tecvayli):** Initially approved for later lines of therapy, recent Phase 3 clinical trial data (such as the MajesTEC-3 trial) has demonstrated substantial progression-free and overall survival benefits when used as early as the second-line setting [search-sourced]. Teclistamab has shown significant promise in reducing the risk of disease progression for patients who have already failed anti-CD38 therapies and lenalidomide [search-sourced].

**4. BCMA-Targeted Antibody-Drug Conjugates**
*   **Belantamab mafodotin (Blenrep):** Based on the phase 3 DREAMM-7 trial data reported in 2024, belantamab mafodotin in combination with bortezomib and dexamethasone (BVd) yielded a significant improvement in overall survival and progression-free survival [search-sourced]. This makes it a highly effective "off-the-shelf" BCMA-targeted option for second-line treatment, reducing the risk of disease progression or death considerably compared to standard comparator regimens [search-sourced].

**Considerations on Anti-CD38 Therapies:** 
While regimens containing isatuximab (e.g., Isa-Kd or Isa-Pd) are approved in the relapsed setting, isatuximab targets the same CD38 receptor as daratumumab [search-sourced]. As a result, clinical experts typically avoid immediately transitioning to it after progression on Dara-VRd, preferring to switch treatment mechanisms entirely to ensure maximum efficacy [search-sourced].
