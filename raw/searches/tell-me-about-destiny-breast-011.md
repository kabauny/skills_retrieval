# Tell me about Destiny breast 011 

**DESTINY-Breast11** (frequently referred to as **DESTINY-Breast011**) is an open-label, multicenter Phase 3 randomized trial evaluating the antibody-drug conjugate trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) as a *neoadjuvant* (pre-surgical) treatment for high-risk HER2-positive early-stage breast cancer [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07239271) [medinews.it](https://www.medinews.it/newsletter/aiomnews-n-1027-25-novembre-2025/). 

### Trial Design and Key Findings
The study was designed to investigate whether a T-DXd-based regimen could outperform the standard of care and potentially allow patients to safely omit anthracyclines [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07239271) [medpagetoday.com](https://www.medpagetoday.com/meetingcoverage/esmo/118025). Patients were randomized into three arms:
1. T-DXd monotherapy [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07239271) [medinews.it](https://www.medinews.it/newsletter/aiomnews-n-1027-25-novembre-2025/)
2. T-DXd followed by THP (paclitaxel, trastuzumab, and pertuzumab) [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07239271) [medinews.it](https://www.medinews.it/newsletter/aiomnews-n-1027-25-novembre-2025/)
3. Standard of care (SoC): dose-dense doxorubicin and cyclophosphamide (ddAC) followed by THP [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07239271) [medinews.it](https://www.medinews.it/newsletter/aiomnews-n-1027-25-novembre-2025/)

The primary endpoint of the study is pathologic complete response (pCR), which measures whether the tumor is completely eradicated prior to surgery [clinicaltrials.gov](https://clinicaltrials.gov/study/NCT07239271) [medinews.it](https://www.medinews.it/newsletter/aiomnews-n-1027-25-novembre-2025/). 

According to results presented in late 2025, the anthracycline-free regimen of T-DXd followed by THP significantly improved pCR rates compared to the standard ddAC-THP regimen [medpagetoday.com](https://www.medpagetoday.com/meetingcoverage/esmo/118025). 
* **Subgroup Consistency:** The improvement in pCR was seen in both hormone receptor-positive (61.4% vs. 52.3%) and hormone receptor-negative (83.1% vs. 67.1%) patient subgroups [medpagetoday.com](https://www.medpagetoday.com/meetingcoverage/esmo/118025).
* **Residual Disease:** Following surgery, 81.3% of patients treated in the T-DXd-THP arm had no or minimal residual invasive cancer, compared to 69.1% of patients in the control arm [medpagetoday.com](https://www.medpagetoday.com/meetingcoverage/esmo/118025).
* **Event-Free Survival (EFS):** Investigators also noted an early, favorable trend in EFS for the T-DXd-THP arm (HR 0.56) [medpagetoday.com](https://www.medpagetoday.com/meetingcoverage/esmo/118025).

### Context Within the HER2+ Treatment Paradigm
Currently, the standard neoadjuvant regimen for higher-stage HER2+ breast cancer typically involves chemotherapy plus dual HER2 blockade (such as the TCHP regimen) [[her2-positive-breast-cancer]]. By significantly improving upfront pCR rates, DESTINY-Breast11 positions T-DXd to potentially reshape this initial standard of care [medpagetoday.com](https://www.medpagetoday.com/meetingcoverage/esmo/118025). 

This trial is the pre-surgical companion to its sister study, **DESTINY-Breast05**, which evaluates T-DXd in the *adjuvant* (post-surgical) setting [[destiny-breast05]]. Together, they dictate care based on the patient's surgical pathology:
* Patients who achieve a **pCR** after neoadjuvant therapy continue with standard HER2 blockade (trastuzumab +/- pertuzumab) to complete one year of total therapy [[adjuvant-her2-positive-breast-cancer]], [[her2-positive-breast-cancer]].
* Patients who **fail** to achieve a pCR have "residual invasive disease," making them a higher-risk group. Currently, the standard of care for these patients is to switch to trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1) based on the `KATHERINE trial` [[katherine-trial]], [[adjuvant-her2-positive-breast-cancer]].
* However, DESTINY-Breast05 proved that using T-DXd instead of T-DM1 in this residual-disease setting reduces the risk of recurrence events by 53% [[destiny-breast05]]. Adjuvant T-DXd is widely expected to supersede T-DM1 and has a PDUFA target action date of July 7, 2026 [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]].

For both the neoadjuvant (DESTINY-Breast11) and adjuvant (DESTINY-Breast05) settings, the principal toxicity concern when incorporating T-DXd is the risk of interstitial lung disease (ILD) or pneumonitis, which requires proactive serial imaging and prompt steroid management [[trastuzumab-deruxtecan]], [[destiny-breast05-april-2026-update]].
