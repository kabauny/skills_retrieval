# latest advances in ADC therapy for breast cancer 2026

Recent advances in Antibody-Drug Conjugate (ADC) therapy for breast cancer in 2026 have been marked by significant clinical trial results and several FDA approvals, expanding treatment options across various subtypes of the disease. These developments highlight the growing role of ADCs in earlier lines of therapy and for a broader range of patients.

**Key Clinical Trial Results and FDA Approvals:**

*   **HER2-Positive Breast Cancer:**
    *   **Trastuzumab deruxtecan (T-DXd) plus pertuzumab (Perjeta)** received FDA approval in December 2025 as a preferred first-line treatment for unresectable or metastatic HER2-positive breast cancer. This approval was based on the DESTINY-Breast09 clinical trial, which demonstrated superior efficacy compared to the previous standard taxane-based CLEOPATRA regimen. [oncologynewscentral.com](https://www.oncologynewscentral.com/breast-cancer/after-fda-approves-adc-combo-big-questions-remain-in-her2-positive-breast-cancer) [aacr.org](https://www.aacr.org/blog/2026/01/06/fda-approvals-in-oncology-october-december-2025/) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/the-top-breast-cancer-news-of-2025)
    *   For patients with early-stage HER2-positive breast cancer who have residual invasive disease after neoadjuvant HER2-directed therapy, **T-DXd** has shown promising results. The Phase 3 DESTINY-Breast05 trial, with results presented in October 2025, indicated a 53% reduction in the risk of disease progression or death compared to ado-trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1). [esmo.org](https://www.esmo.org/press-releases/new-generation-of-antibody-drug-conjugates-adcs-shows-unprecedented-promise-in-early-stage-disease) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-flashback-breast-cancer-decisions-and-news-from-march-2026) A supplemental biologics license application (sBLA) for T-DXd in this setting was granted priority review by the FDA in March 2026, with a Prescription Drug User Fee Act (PDUFA) target action date of July 7, 2026. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-flashback-breast-cancer-decisions-and-news-from-march-2026)
    *   The DESTINY-Breast11 study, presented at the 2025 ESMO Congress, supported the sBLA of T-DXd followed by paclitaxel, trastuzumab, and pertuzumab for the neoadjuvant treatment of adult patients with HER2-positive stage 2 or 3 breast cancer. [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/7-upcoming-fda-decisions-in-q2-2026-to-watch) Patients treated with this regimen experienced an improved pathologic complete response rate of 67.3% compared to 56.3% with the conventional doxorubicin and cyclophosphamide plus THP regimen. [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/7-upcoming-fda-decisions-in-q2-2026-to-watch)

*   **Triple-Negative Breast Cancer (TNBC):**
    *   **Sacituzumab govitecan (Trodelvy) plus pembrolizumab** demonstrated durable responses and improved progression-free survival (PFS) in patients with advanced triple-negative breast cancer, according to results from the ASCENT-04/KEYNOTE-D19 study published in January 2026. [dana-farber.org](https://www.dana-farber.org/newsroom/news-releases/2026/adc-combination-outperforms-standard-treatment-of-advanced-triple-negative-breast-cancer) This combination is anticipated to be submitted to regulators and could change clinical practice for first-line treatment of PD-L1-positive advanced TNBC. [dana-farber.org](https://www.dana-farber.org/newsroom/news-releases/2026/adc-combination-outperforms-standard-treatment-of-advanced-triple-negative-breast-cancer)
    *   **Datopotamab deruxtecan (Dato-DXd; Datroway)** received priority review from the FDA in February 2026 for the first-line treatment of adult patients with unresectable or metastatic TNBC who are not eligible for PD-1 or PD-L1 inhibitor therapy. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/fda-flashback-breast-cancer-decisions-and-news-from-march-2026) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/7-upcoming-fda-decisions-in-q2-2026-to-watch) The pivotal Phase 3 TROPION-Breast02 clinical trial supported this application, showing a median overall survival of 23.7 months for Dato-DXd compared to 18.7 months for chemotherapy. [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/7-upcoming-fda-decisions-in-q2-2026-to-watch)
    *   The ASCENT-03 and TROPION-Breast02 trials, presented at the ESMO Congress 2025, confirmed the efficacy of TROP2-directed ADCs (sacituzumab govitecan and datopotamab deruxtecan, respectively) with or without immunotherapy in first-line metastatic TNBC, particularly for patients not candidates for immune checkpoint inhibition. [ascopubs.org](https://dailynews.ascopubs.org/do/using-antibody-drug-conjugates-frontline-setting-treatment-updates-metastatic-triple) [youtube.com](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dGvRugIhcd8)
    *   **Izalontamab brengitecan (BL-B01D1)**, a HER3- and EGFR-directed bispecific ADC, met its dual primary endpoints in a Phase 3 trial (BL-B01D1-307) by significantly improving PFS and overall survival versus single-agent chemotherapy options in taxane-pretreated unresectable or metastatic TNBC. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/research-rewind-phase-3-breast-cancer-data-highlights-from-quarter-1-of-2026) Full trial findings are expected to be shared at an upcoming medical meeting. [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/research-rewind-phase-3-breast-cancer-data-highlights-from-quarter-1-of-2026)

*   **Hormone Receptor-Positive (HR+), HER2-Negative Breast Cancer:**
    *   **Datopotamab deruxtecan (Dato-DXd; Datroway)** was approved by the FDA in January 2025 for adult patients with unresectable or metastatic HR+/HER2- breast cancer who have received prior endocrine-based therapy and chemotherapy. [pharmacytimes.com](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/2025-fda-approvals-highlight-the-growing-role-of-biomarkers-in-breast-cancer) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/the-top-breast-cancer-news-of-2025) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/december-10-2025/new-fda-approved-oncology-drugs-and-label-updates-between-december-1-2024-and-november-19-2025/) This approval was based on the Phase 3 TROPION-Breast01 trial, which demonstrated improved PFS and overall response rates. [pharmacytimes.com](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/2025-fda-approvals-highlight-the-growing-role-of-biomarkers-in-breast-cancer) [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/the-top-breast-cancer-news-of-2025)

*   **Emerging ADCs and Designations:**
    *   **Datroway (datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk)** was approved by the FDA in 2025 to treat certain patients with advanced breast cancer, representing a new generation of targeted cancer medicines. [susupport.com](https://www.susupport.com/blogs/biopharmaceutical-products/fda-approved-antibody-drug-conjugates-adcs)
    *   **Emiltatug ledadotin (Emi-Le)**, a B7-H4-targeting ADC, received fast-track designation from the FDA in December 2025 for patients with advanced or metastatic breast cancer, including HER2-low or HER2-negative disease (including TNBC), who have been previously treated with a topoisomerase-1 inhibitor ADC. [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/the-top-breast-cancer-news-of-2025) Initial Phase 1 data showed a 23% confirmed overall response rate in B7-H4 high tumors. [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/the-top-breast-cancer-news-of-2025)

These advancements underscore a paradigm shift towards incorporating ADCs earlier in the treatment landscape and tailoring therapies based on specific breast cancer subtypes and biomarker expression.
