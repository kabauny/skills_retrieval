# Adjuvant chemotherapy vs ovarian function suppression with aromatase inhibitor in premenopausal HR+/HER2- intermediate Oncotype DX recurrence score node-negative breast cancer 2025-2026

The management of premenopausal, hormone receptor-positive (HR+), HER2-negative breast cancer with an intermediate Oncotype DX recurrence score (RS) of 16-25 and node-negative disease remains a complex area, with ongoing debate regarding the role of adjuvant chemotherapy versus ovarian function suppression (OFS) with an aromatase inhibitor (AI). Recent updates from landmark trials like RxPONDER and TAILORx, alongside evolving guideline recommendations and emerging ctDNA data, continue to refine treatment paradigms in 2025-2026.

**1. The RxPONDER Premenopausal Subset Debate: Chemotherapy Benefit and Ovarian Suppression**

The RxPONDER trial, which evaluated patients with HR+/HER2- breast cancer and 1-3 positive lymph nodes (pN1), demonstrated a significant benefit from adjuvant chemotherapy in premenopausal women with an Oncotype DX RS of 0-25, unlike their postmenopausal counterparts [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/january-25-2022/rxponder-update-explores-benefit-of-chemotherapy-in-subgroups/) [clinicaloncology.com](https://www.clinicaloncology.com/Vogl-NY/Article/02-21/RxPonder-Changes-the-Field-for-Premenopausal-Breast-Cancer-Patients-With-Involved-Nodes/62636) [valuebasedcancer.com](https://www.valuebasedcancer.com/issues/2021/june-2021-vol-12-no-3/adjuvant-chemotherapy-may-be-beneficial-in-premenopausal-women-with-hr-positive-early-stage-breast-cancer) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11976225/) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918761/). Specifically, premenopausal women treated with chemo-endocrine therapy showed a 5-year invasive disease-free survival (iDFS) of 93.9% compared to 89.0% with endocrine therapy alone, representing a 40% risk reduction in an iDFS event [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918761/).

A central debate revolves around whether this chemotherapy benefit is a true cytotoxic effect or largely mediated by chemotherapy-induced ovarian suppression (CIOS) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/january-25-2022/rxponder-update-explores-benefit-of-chemotherapy-in-subgroups/) [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/ovarian-function-suppression-drives-chemotherapy-benefits-in-premenopausal-hr-her2--breast-cancer) [valuebasedcancer.com](https://www.valuebasedcancer.com/issues/2021/june-2021-vol-12-no-3/adjuvant-chemotherapy-may-be-beneficial-in-premenopausal-women-with-hr-positive-early-stage-breast-cancer) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/february-10-2022/rxponder-trial-another-step-in-defining-which-patients-with-breast-cancer-may-be-spared-adjuvant-cytotoxic-chemotherapy/) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918761/). Dr. Kevin Kalinsky, a lead investigator for RxPONDER, has argued that ovarian function suppression plays a significant role, noting that patients who achieved amenorrhea (cessation of regular periods) had better outcomes regardless of the initial therapy [onclive.com](https://www.onclive.com/view/ovarian-function-suppression-drives-chemotherapy-benefits-in-premenopausal-hr-her2--breast-cancer). However, the initial RxPONDER analysis could not definitively establish that chemotherapy's benefit was solely due to ovarian suppression, as ovarian suppression rates remained relatively low and consistent in both arms over time [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/january-25-2022/rxponder-update-explores-benefit-of-chemotherapy-in-subgroups/).

A significant update from the RxPONDER trial presented in June 2024 explored the role of baseline serum anti-Müllerian hormone (AMH) levels. This post-hoc analysis found that premenopausal women with low AMH levels (indicating lower ovarian reserve) benefited less from adjuvant chemotherapy compared to those with medium or high AMH levels. [ascopubs.org](https://dailynews.ascopubs.org/do/rxponder-finds-anti-m-x00fc-llerian-hormone-levels-may-predict-chemotherapy-response) This finding supports the hypothesis that the extent of ovarian function, and its suppression, contributes to the observed chemotherapy benefit. The ongoing phase 3 OFSET trial (NCT05879926) is specifically designed to address this by comparing OFS + endocrine therapy (ET) versus chemotherapy + OFS + ET in premenopausal women, including those with intermediate RS (node-negative) and low RS (node-positive), with results anticipated to provide further clarity. [ascopubs.org](https://dailynews.ascopubs.org/do/rxponder-finds-anti-m-x00fc-llerian-hormone-levels-may-predict-chemotherapy-response) [ascopubs.org](https://ascopubs.org/doi/10.1200/JCO.2025.43.16_suppl.TPS615)

**2. TAILORx Intermediate-RS Subgroup Analyses and High-Risk Clinical Features**

The TAILORx trial focused on HR+/HER2- node-negative breast cancer. Its primary findings indicated that most women with an intermediate RS (11-25) could safely forgo chemotherapy and be treated with endocrine therapy alone. [ecancer.org](https://ecancer.org/en/news/16128-asco-2019-new-tailorx-data-guides-adjuvant-therapy-in-younger-breast-cancer-patients) [breastcancer.org](https://www.breastcancer.org/research-news/updated-tailorx-results-confirm-women-with-intermediate-recurrence-score-can-skip-chemotherapy) [nam.edu](https://nam.edu/perspectives/breast-cancer-management-in-the-tailorx-era-less-is-more/) [advocacy-ecog-acrin.org](https://www.advocacy-ecog-acrin.org/trial-results-the-tailorx-trial-continues-to-provide-reassurance-that-many-women-with-early-breast-cancer-can-safely-forego-chemotherapy/) [ecog-acrin.org](https://ecog-acrin.org/press-release-tailorx-trial-finds-most-women-with-early-breast-cancer-do-not-benefit-from-chemotherapy/) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/news/december-2022/tailorx-update-12-year-recurrence-and-survival-outcomes-for-patients-with-early-stage-breast-cancer/) However, a crucial subgroup analysis revealed that premenopausal women (defined as age ≤50 years) with an RS of 16-25, and particularly those with high-risk clinical features (such as Grade 3 tumor, larger tumor size, or younger age), *did* derive a small but significant benefit from adjuvant chemotherapy. T [ecancer.org](https://ecancer.org/en/news/16128-asco-2019-new-tailorx-data-guides-adjuvant-therapy-in-younger-breast-cancer-patients) [ascopubs.org](https://ascopubs.org/doi/abs/10.1200/PO.23.00390) [nam.edu](https://nam.edu/perspectives/breast-cancer-management-in-the-tailorx-era-less-is-more/) [advocacy-ecog-acrin.org](https://www.advocacy-ecog-acrin.org/trial-results-the-tailorx-trial-continues-to-provide-reassurance-that-many-women-with-early-breast-cancer-can-safely-forego-chemotherapy/) [mayo.edu](https://ce.mayo.edu/sites/default/files/Chemo%20Benefit%20%26%20Clinical%20Risk%20-%20EXS11936_0420.pdf) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11982449/) [ecog-acrin.org](https://ecog-acrin.org/press-release-tailorx-trial-finds-most-women-with-early-breast-cancer-do-not-benefit-from-chemotherapy/) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/news/december-2022/tailorx-update-12-year-recurrence-and-survival-outcomes-for-patients-with-early-stage-breast-cancer/)his benefit for younger women with higher intermediate RS persisted even with 12 years of follow-up data. T [advocacy-ecog-acrin.org](https://www.advocacy-ecog-acrin.org/trial-results-the-tailorx-trial-continues-to-provide-reassurance-that-many-women-with-early-breast-cancer-can-safely-forego-chemotherapy/) [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/news/december-2022/tailorx-update-12-year-recurrence-and-survival-outcomes-for-patients-with-early-stage-breast-cancer/)his suggests that for premenopausal women in this specific risk group, chemotherapy's impact, potentially including a castration effect, remains clinically relevant. A [ecancer.org](https://ecancer.org/en/news/16128-asco-2019-new-tailorx-data-guides-adjuvant-therapy-in-younger-breast-cancer-patients) retrospective analysis using the National Cancer Database in April 2024 further supported an overall survival benefit for adjuvant chemotherapy in premenopausal node-negative patients aged under 50 with an intermediate RS, especially for scores 21-25.

 [ascopubs.org](https://ascopubs.org/doi/abs/10.1200/PO.23.00390) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11000770/)**3. Current NCCN/ASCO/ESMO Guidance on OFS + AI as an Alternative to Adjuvant Chemotherapy**

Current guidelines acknowledge the complexity in premenopausal patients.
*   **NCCN Guidelines (2025-2026):** For premenopausal, node-negative patients with an Oncotype DX RS of 16-25, the NCCN guidelines (as discussed in September 2025) suggest that chemotherapy *could* be offered, and is generally offered, given that a benefit could not be ruled out in the TAILORx premenopausal subgroup. F [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/applying-nccn-guidelines-for-pre--and-post-menopausal-breast-cancer)or those with an RS of 15 or below, adjuvant endocrine therapy with or without ovarian suppression is typically recommended without chemotherapy. T [targetedonc.com](https://www.targetedonc.com/view/applying-nccn-guidelines-for-pre--and-post-menopausal-breast-cancer)he NCCN guidelines also recommend adjuvant endocrine therapy for premenopausal women, which can include tamoxifen (with or without OFS) or an AI (with OFS) for 5 years.
* [jons-online.com](https://www.jons-online.com/articles/updated-nccn-breast-cancer-treatment-guideline-reflects-expanding-treatment-options-for-hr-positive-her2-negative-disease)   **ASCO/ESMO:** While specific 2025-2026 ASCO/ESMO guidelines focused on *OFS+AI as a direct alternative to chemotherapy* for *intermediate RS node-negative premenopausal women* were not explicitly detailed in the search results, the benefit of OFS has been clearly established in other contexts. The SOFT and TEXT trials demonstrated that OFS plus exemestane (an AI) significantly reduced recurrence compared to tamoxifen plus OFS in high-risk premenopausal women. E [oncodaily.com](https://oncodaily.com/oncolibrary/breast-oncology/early-luminal-breast-cancer) [jons-online.com](https://www.jons-online.com/articles/updated-nccn-breast-cancer-treatment-guideline-reflects-expanding-treatment-options-for-hr-positive-her2-negative-disease) [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4711215/) [dana-farber.org](https://www.dana-farber.org/newsroom/news-releases/2026/young-women-with-early-stage-breast-cancer-can-benefit-from-extending-post-treatment-hormone-therapy-beyond-five-years)xperts in 2022 noted that OFS has a "clearly proven benefit" in adjuvant therapy for young women, highlighting the need for prospective trials to determine if OFS can replace chemotherapy in node-positive, low genomic risk premenopausal women. A [ascopost.com](https://ascopost.com/issues/february-10-2022/rxponder-trial-another-step-in-defining-which-patients-with-breast-cancer-may-be-spared-adjuvant-cytotoxic-chemotherapy/) 2026 practical guide emphasizes that OFS should not be underused in premenopausal disease. T [oncodaily.com](https://oncodaily.com/oncolibrary/breast-oncology/early-luminal-breast-cancer)he general approach in premenopausal patients with HR+ disease and indications for ovarian suppression is to use OFS in conjunction with either tamoxifen or an AI (the latter always requiring OFS). T [aacrjournals.org](https://aacrjournals.org/clincancerres/article/24/21/5206/281649/Targeted-Therapy-for-Premenopausal-Women-with-HR) [jons-online.com](https://www.jons-online.com/articles/updated-nccn-breast-cancer-treatment-guideline-reflects-expanding-treatment-options-for-hr-positive-her2-negative-disease)he decision between chemotherapy and intensified endocrine therapy (including OFS + AI) in the intermediate RS group for premenopausal patients often involves integrating nodal burden and tumor features with genomic data.

TAILORx's 12-year data reaffirms its initial findings but continues to highlight the chemotherapy benefit in the specific premenopausal, intermediate-to-higher RS subgroup. Extrapolation from Postmenopausal Data**

Treatment strategies for HR+, HER2- breast cancer in premenopausal women have historically been, and continue to be, *extrapolated from data primarily derived from postmenopausal patients*, with the addition of OFS to endocrine therapy. T [aacrjournals.org](https://aacrjournals.org/clincancerres/article/24/21/5206/281649/Targeted-Therapy-for-Premenopausal-Women-with-HR) [fda.gov](https://www.fda.gov/media/142638/download)he rationale is to achieve a similar estrogen-deprived state. However, it is recognized that premenopausal breast cancer often exhibits different tumor characteristics, such as a more advanced stage at diagnosis, higher tumor grades, larger tumor sizes, and more positive lymph nodes. A [aacrjournals.org](https://aacrjournals.org/clincancerres/article/24/21/5206/281649/Targeted-Therapy-for-Premenopausal-Women-with-HR) March 2025 study compared oncological outcomes of premenopausal women with OFS to postmenopausal women, finding comparable 10-year iDFS and distant recurrence-free survival (DRFS) rates in the cohort not receiving chemotherapy. T [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11976225/)his suggests that achieving adequate estrogen suppression in premenopausal women can lead to similar outcomes as in postmenopausal women, supporting the principle of OFS+AI in selected cases.

**6. Distinguishing Premenopausal from Perimenopausal Patients**

Distinguishing premenopausal from perimenopausal status is critical for treatment decisions. While clinical features like age and menstrual history are commonly used, biochemical markers provide more definitive classification.
*   **RxPONDER:** In RxPONDER, patients who self-reported as perimenopausal were classified as premenopausal by study definition. T [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918761/)he trial has since refined its definitions using a combination of age, last menstrual period, and serum FSH data.
* [nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918761/)   **Biochemical Assessment:** The June 2024 RxPONDER update emphasized that AMH levels are a stronger predictor of chemotherapy response than self-reported menopausal status or age. F [ascopubs.org](https://dailynews.ascopubs.org/do/rxponder-finds-anti-m-x00fc-llerian-hormone-levels-may-predict-chemotherapy-response)or clinical practice, documentation for premenopausal status in some contexts may require amenorrhea for more than 12 months (in the absence of confounding treatments) and FSH and estradiol levels in the postmenopausal range.

 [healthhelp.com](https://www.healthhelp.com/wp-content/uploads/P_967.pdf)In summary, for premenopausal, HR+/HER2- intermediate Oncotype DX recurrence score node-negative breast cancer, the decision to use adjuvant chemotherapy or OFS + AI remains nuanced. While TAILORx suggests chemotherapy benefit in certain high-risk premenopausal patients with intermediate RS, the underlying mechanism, particularly the role of CIOS, continues to be debated and is a focus of ongoing research (e.g., OFSET trial). Current guidelines incorporate menopausal status, and OFS + AI is a well-established strategy for effective endocrine control, often adopted from postmenopausal data. Emerging ctDNA data holds promise for future personalized approaches but is not yet standard clinical practice for treatment decisions.
